Transcription

Transcription is turning something spoken into something written; in our case, writing down the melody of a hymn we hear. Transcription is a powerful exercise for developing the ear because it forces us to listen carefully.

A few things are helpful when beginning transcription:

  • Find recordings of hymns that are slower and less embellished, as you will have an easier time discerning the melody.
  • To help you determine which notes you are hearing, use a byzantine music app (byzison.xyz) as a reference (make sure to set the pitch setting to match the recording).
  • Copy out the lyrics, and write the notes above each syllable. Use the symbols from the martyria (c v b n m , .), not the uppercase symbols that are used notate the ison (Ν Π Β Γ Δ Κ Ζ). Let's not confuse ourselves.
  • Don't worry about writing the melodies in Byzantine notation yet - that is a more advanced skill.

In the beginning you may feel quite unsure and be very slow. This is normal. Persist!

Exercises

Χριστός Ανέστη (the Paschal Troparion)

Χριστὸς ἀνέστη ἐκ νεκρῶν,
θανάτῳ θάνατον πατήσας,
καὶ τοῖς ἐν τοῖς μνήμασι,
ζωὴν χαρισάμενος!

Christós anésti ek nekrón,
thanáto thánaton patísas,
ké tís en tís mnímasi,
zoín charisámenos!

Here is a slow, plain recording. Both the ison and the first note ("Χρι") are Πα. Set byzison.xyz to pitch -3.
