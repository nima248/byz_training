Aural Foundations for Byzantine Chant

Welcome

Learning to chant is a beautiful and rewarding journey. For some it can be a confusing and disheartening journey because they are not taught how to listen like a musician (indeed church chanters are not just musicians, but they are musicians). This site is here to help you develop your aural skill, that is, the skill of listening and understanding music. The effort you put in here, with God's help, will reward you with a deeper intuitive understanding of not only Byzantine music, but music in general, and will make you a more confident and capable chanter.

Listening is the foundational skill of music

We are not born able to speak our native language, but we learn it gradually through consistent exposure. A child learns to speak clearly because they spend years listening to language before producing it themselves.

Music is learned in much the same way as language. Before we can produce music well, we must first learn to hear and understand it internally. Listening comes first, then understanding, and finally confident expression.

Some musicians get a kind of shortcut when learning music. For example, a beginner pianist can learn quite quickly to play a pleasant tune just by pressing the right keys, even with limited listening skills. Chanters get no such "free lunch". Our instrument is not just our voice, but our voice and our ear working together to sing at the correct pitch. We can only be as good at chanting as we are at hearing.

Thus beginning chanting can feel slow and confusing. This is normal. It is not a matter of "natural talent", but of learnable skills. Everyone begins life with zero musical skill. Everyone who practices consistently will improve over time.

Byzantine Ear Training

This site aims to train the "ear" - not the things on the sides of our head, but the internal ear, our mental understanding of the music we hear. Ear training develops listening skills and musical intuition using structured exercises. It trains foundational skills such as:

  • Singing in tune with a drone (ison)
  • Hearing melodic movement
  • Internalising pitch before singing
  • Singing pitch accurately
  • Recognising small differences in pitch
  • Maintaining stable pitch over time
All chanters have developed these skills to varying degrees. Some developed them well from a young age through years of exposure to chanting and other music, along with guidance and encouragement from mature musicians. Others may not have had this benefit, and have significant gaps. No matter where our skill level currently sits, beginner to master, ear training can always be used to help us grow stronger.

Most ear training programs use western theory, including chords and western interval names, which are unnecessary for students of Byzantine chant. Here the exercises are designed for chanters, and use the Byzantine parallagi:

Νη Πα Βου Γα Δι Κε Ζω Νη

Ni Pa Vou Ga Dhi Ke Zo Ni

Authentic Chanting Relies on Tradition

Byzantine chant is an oral (as in "spoken") tradition, passed down through the life of the Orthodox church. To learn chanting properly, every student requires an experienced teacher and many hours both in the classroom and at the analogion. This website is intended only as a complement to lessons and mentorship, not as a replacement.

Additionally, a complete Byzantine chant ear training program must include lots of listening to skilled traditional chanters. Many aspects of style and expression can only be learned by absorbing authentic chanting. Here are just a few good traditional chanters whose recordings are worth studying:

  • Panagiotis Neochoritis (Παναγιώτης Νεοχωρίτης)
  • Dimos Papatzalakis (Δήμος Παπατζαλάκης)
  • Dimosthenis Paikopoulos (Δημοσθένης Παϊκόπουλος)
  • Ioannis Liakos (Ιωάννης Λιάκος)
