Exercises

The path to improvement is consistent practice. It will be difficult at first. Everything is difficult until it becomes easy with practice. If you trust the process and practice consistently, you will improve. The basic routine is done in less than 10 minutes:

  • Warm up (2 min)
  • One exercise (~6 min)

How to warm up

Get in the habit of always warming up your voice properly before chanting. The voice is fragile, and if injured it can be very difficult to restore. Even without significant other practice, doing a regular vocal warm up will build your vocal strength and stamina. If you don't have time for any other practice, still do a warm up at least once a day. Once you know how you can do it anywhere.

Suggested 2 minute warm up

  • Three gentle neck rolls (10s)
  • Gently massage the front of your neck and larynx (10s)
  • Two big yawns. Focus on opening the mouth and throat wide and lowering your larynx (feel your Adam's apple), and loosening the jaw. (10s)
  • Two deep inhalations into your belly with slow exhales (15s)
  • Vocal sirens. Hum gently and slide up and down within a comfortable range. (40s)
  • Lip trills. Same as sirens, but add in buzzing the lips. Gently increase the pitch range and volume. (35s)
For longer practice sessions, spend more time warming up.

How to do exercises

During exercises, follow the instructions.

  • Listen: Just listen! No humming.
  • Audiate: Read below.
  • Sing: Sing by yourself.
  • Together: Sing along and match what you hear.

Audiation is hearing music in your mind without any external sound. No chanting or humming allowed - only hearing mentally. It is not literally hallucinating sounds, but more like a mental awareness, a knowing of what the sound is. You probably audiate when you read - you can hear, in some sense, what the words sound like, and imagine that someone is saying them.

At first audiation will feel difficult, and like you're doing it wrong. This is normal, and you're not doing it wrong - you're laying a foundation. Just relax and trust the process. Resist the temptation to sing or hum to find the pitch when audiating. That's cheating, and it will slow your progress.
